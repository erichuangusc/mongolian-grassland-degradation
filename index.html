Abstract

Grassland degradation on the Mongolian Plateau is accelerating under the combined pressure of climate change and human activities. In this project, we frame the prediction of bare soil one year in advance as a supervised classification problem with a resolution of 10m. For each pixel or 224x224 Sentinel-2 chip, we predict whether it will be bare or non-bare-dominated in year t+1.

Overall, our findings show that short-horizon desertification at 10m resolution is meaningfully predictable. Transformer models (SatMAE) that have representation learning capabilities can substantially outperform traditional feature-engineered classifiers for this task.

Data & Methodology

We define land degradation forecasting as a binary classification problem. We compare Random Forest (feature engineering) against SatMAE (representation learning). The model inputs include multi-season Sentinel-2 mosaics and TerraClimate precipitation statistics.

Key Findings

  • Kubuqi (Deterministic System): The SatMAE forecaster attained 98.4% top-1 accuracy. The system exhibits strong short-term memory, driven by stable dune interiors and engineered shelter belts.
  • East MN (Policy-Driven): Results show a sharp reduction in bare area, consistent with net revegetation policies. Recovery is better explained by human interventions visible in imagery rather than short-term climate anomalies.
